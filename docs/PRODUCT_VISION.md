# Product Vision

## The Problem

Options traders can move from idea to order ticket very quickly. That speed is useful when someone is prepared, but it is dangerous when the contract has not been checked.

Many losing trades are not mysterious after the fact. The warning signs were visible:

- premium risk was too large
- expiration was too close
- the breakeven move was unrealistic
- liquidity was unknown
- IV or earnings risk was ignored
- the trader was chasing instead of following a plan

RiskWise is built around the idea that better decisions often come from slowing down, not from adding another prediction.

## Target Users

RiskWise is mainly for individual options traders and students learning how options risk works.

The first users are not institutions. They are people who want a second set of eyes before they act on a trade idea. Some may be beginners learning calls and puts. Others may already trade options but want stricter position sizing and cleaner pre-trade discipline.

## Why Options Traders Need Pre-Trade Review

Stocks are already risky, but options add more moving parts:

- direction
- time
- volatility
- strike selection
- contract price
- bid/ask spread
- expiration
- position size

A user can be directionally right and still lose money if the contract is expensive, the move is too small, or volatility drops after an event.

RiskWise is meant to catch those questions before the user acts.

## Why Decision Quality Matters More Than Predictions

The app is not trying to say where a stock will go tomorrow.

Instead, it asks:

- Is the risk defined?
- Is the size reasonable?
- Is the thesis clear?
- What evidence is missing?
- What would invalidate the idea?
- Does this trade fit the user's own rules?

That makes RiskWise more like a pre-flight checklist than a trading signal service.

## Product Direction

The core product loop is:

1. Build or import a trade idea.
2. Check contract math and missing evidence.
3. Read a multi-agent investigation.
4. Ask RiskWiseAI follow-up questions.
5. Save useful context for future reviews.

Extra dashboards, news feeds, alerts, and portfolio features can come later. The first version should stay focused on pre-trade investigation.
